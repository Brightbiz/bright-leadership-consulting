Overview

What the Executive Alignment Index Is

The Executive Alignment Index (EAI) is a structured governance instrument that measures executive variance across six critical dimensions. It does not measure sentiment. It measures structural cohesion.

◆

Strategic Interpretation

Consistency in how executives interpret strategic direction

◆

Decision Rights Clarity

Defined ownership of decision authority across functions

◆

Cross-Functional Coordination

Alignment of processes and priorities between divisions

◆

Escalation Pathways

Structured routes for issue resolution and governance

◆

Accountability Architecture

Clarity of ownership, reporting lines, and outcomes

◆

Risk Ownership

Distribution and acceptance of risk at executive level

Methodology

How the EAI Works

Executives are assessed across defined alignment dimensions using a calibrated 1–5 maturity scale. Results are aggregated into a composite alignment score, with variance indicators identifying divergence between executives.

The emphasis is not average agreement — but dispersion and structural misalignment risk.
