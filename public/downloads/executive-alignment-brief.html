Section I

The Alignment Problem

Executive alignment rarely fails abruptly. It erodes through variance in decision architecture, strategic interpretation, and accountability distribution.

Most organisations experience this as operational friction — delayed decisions, duplicated initiatives, escalation bottlenecks. The root cause is rarely capability. It is structural.

In complex organisations, alignment doesn't collapse — it drifts. And drift is invisible until it becomes expensive.

Traditional approaches — engagement surveys, personality profiling, leadership development programmes — measure sentiment or individual capability. They do not diagnose structural coherence at the executive layer.

Section II

The Executive Alignment Index

The Executive Alignment Index is a diagnostic instrument that measures structural cohesion across six governance-critical dimensions.

The Six Dimensions

  • Strategic Interpretation — Are executives interpreting the strategy consistently?
  • Decision Rights — Is it clear who decides what, and when?
  • Cross-Functional Coordination — Are interdependencies managed or assumed?
  • Escalation Pathways — Do escalation routes exist, and are they used appropriately?
  • Accountability Architecture — Is accountability distributed or concentrated?
  • Risk Ownership — Is risk owned at the appropriate level?

Each dimension is scored on a validated 1–5 maturity scale. The Index identifies dispersion — the degree of variance between individual executive scores — as the primary indicator of structural misalignment risk.

Section III

How Engagements Work

Phase 1 — Diagnostic

The Executive Alignment Index is deployed across the senior leadership team. Each executive completes a structured assessment independently. Results are aggregated and analysed for composite alignment and dimensional dispersion.

Phase 2 — Reporting

A board-ready Executive Alignment Index Report is produced. This document provides the composite alignment score, dimension-level breakdown, dispersion mapping, a priority action matrix, and governance risk commentary.

Phase 3 — Advisory

Where commissioned, a structured advisory engagement follows. This may include executive architecture review, role mandate clarification, governance interface design, or succession risk assessment.

Engagements are typically commissioned by the CEO, Chair, or Chief People Officer. Duration and scope are determined during an initial confidential conversation.

Section IV

When Organisations Commission This Work

The Executive Alignment Index is typically deployed during periods of structural transition or strategic recalibration.

  • Strategic planning cycles requiring executive coherence
  • Post-merger integration of leadership teams
  • CEO or Chair transitions
  • Rapid growth outpacing leadership structure
  • Digital or AI transformation requiring governance clarity
  • Board-level concern about executive effectiveness

The question is not whether your executives are capable. The question is whether they are structurally aligned.
