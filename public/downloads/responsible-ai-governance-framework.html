Responsible AI Governance Framework

A structured approach to ethical, transparent, and accountable AI deployment

1. Core Principles

Fairness & Non-Discrimination

AI systems must be designed and monitored to prevent bias across protected characteristics. Regular fairness audits are mandatory.

Transparency & Explainability

Decision-making processes must be interpretable. Stakeholders should understand how and why AI-driven decisions are made.

Accountability & Oversight

Clear ownership for every AI system. Human oversight is required for high-stakes decisions. Escalation paths must be documented.

Privacy & Data Protection

AI systems must comply with data protection regulations (GDPR, POPIA, etc.). Data minimisation and purpose limitation apply.

Safety & Reliability

AI systems must be tested for robustness, security, and edge-case behaviour before deployment. Continuous monitoring is required.

Human Agency

AI augments human judgement; it does not replace it. Users must retain the ability to override, question, and disengage AI systems.

2. AI Risk Classification

All AI use cases must be classified before development or procurement. Classification determines governance requirements.

Risk LevelDescriptionExamplesGovernance Required
HighDecisions affecting individuals' rights, safety, or livelihoodsHiring algorithms, credit scoring, medical diagnosisFull ethical review, human-in-the-loop, ongoing audit
MediumMaterial business impact or customer-facing decisionsPricing optimisation, customer segmentation, demand forecastingImpact assessment, bias testing, quarterly review
LowInternal productivity or non-sensitive applicationsMeeting scheduling, document summarisation, code assistanceStandard IT review, usage guidelines
